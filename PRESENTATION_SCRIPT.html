Section 3

Pipeline animation ~30s

🖥 Screen: switch to landing page, scroll to Pipeline section, let animation play
"Here's the pipeline. Audio capture under 5 milliseconds. openai-whisper transcribes in around 150. Google Translate fires in under 100 — sent immediately as the fast packet. Llama 3.3 70B refines the translation in under 500. pykakasi generates romaji in under 5 milliseconds. Total: under 800 milliseconds. The suggestion agents — CrewAI — run deferred so they never block the translation from appearing."

Section 4

AMD MI300X section ~25s

🖥 Screen: scroll to AMD MI300X section — hero banner + comparison table visible
"Why MI300X? 70 billion parameters at full FP16 needs 140 gigabytes of VRAM. An RTX 4090 has 24. An A100 has 80. The MI300X has 192 gigabytes of HBM3 — Llama 3.3 70B fits in a single GPU, no quantization, no sharding."
"Japanese is precision-sensitive: honorifics, register, verb endings — they degrade at INT4. Full FP16 is the only correct choice for quality translation. That's why this hardware matters."

Section 5

Engineering — Solo banner + architecture diagram ~60s

🖥 Screen: scroll to Engineering section — solo build banner visible
"Built solo in 7 days. Five runtimes: TypeScript for the React overlay, Python for the FastAPI server, Rust for the Tauri backend, JavaScript for the Discord bot, and GLSL for the WebGL shaders on this page."
👆 Scroll down to the 3-step architecture journey
"The architecture in three steps. Step one — the Node.js Discord bot captures each person's voice as a separate Opus stream and forwards it over an encrypted SSH tunnel to the AMD cloud. No public ports. No exposed endpoints."
"Step two — the AMD MI300X: openai-whisper transcribes to Japanese with word-level timestamps. Then the two-pass split — Google Translate fires immediately, Llama 3.3 70B refines at 500ms. pykakasi generates romaji. CrewAI suggestion agents run deferred."
"Step three — WebSocket JSON arrives at the Tauri overlay on your local machine and the card renders. Transparent, always-on-top, over your game."
👆 Point to the Step 02 detail panel (arch-zoom)
"Zooming into step two: the key design is the split. Pass one and pass two run independently. You see a result in 100 milliseconds. You get a quality result at 500. The card updates in place. Perceived latency is always under 100 milliseconds."

Section 6

Engineering decisions — 6 cards ~30s

🖥 Screen: scroll to the 6 engineering decision cards
"Six decisions that defined the architecture:"
"01 — Two-pass translation. Perceived latency under 100ms even though LLM inference takes 500."
"02 — Tauri, not Electron. 50 megabyte binary, native always-on-top, Rust security sandbox."
"03 — SSH tunnel, not public port. Zero exposed endpoints. Same path in dev and prod."
"04 — Per-speaker audio. Mixing destroys speaker identity before transcription. Separate Opus streams per user ID."
"05 — FP16, not quantized. 192 gigabytes fits the model without precision loss. Japanese honorifics degrade at INT4."
"06 — WebSocket, not polling. Karaoke timestamps need real-time delivery. HTTP polling can't achieve sub-frame accuracy."
